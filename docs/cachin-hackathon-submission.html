Project Description

Latin America is QR-first, but foreign money is not. Tourists, foreigners, digital nomads, and freelancers paid in crypto often arrive with dollars, euros, cards, or crypto balances, while local merchants expect QR payments.

Cachin lets users fund a mobile wallet globally and pay locally across LATAM QR rails. The first MVP/proof wedge is Argentina: a user can add funds through crypto, card, SWIFT, or IBAN, scan a supported local QR such as Mercado Pago or MODO, review the FX/fees/final total, and complete the payment. The merchant does not need to change behavior for the basic flow.

If a merchant onboards into Cachin, they unlock the enhanced layer: lower fees, instant settlement, discounts, POS tools, and direct merchant-side confirmation. Founder-provided merchant economics: card fees can range from 3-7%, and settlement can take 15-30 days unless merchants pay more.

The product is LATAM-first. Argentina is the clearest initial wedge because QR payments, foreigner payment friction, and exchange-rate differences are highly visible. Founder-provided Argentina example: card dollar around 1365 versus crypto dollar around 1470. P2P.me is the live demo rail today because it operates without a paid license; Manteca is a planned scale-up path for broader LATAM coverage, contingent on funding the paid provider tier.

Cachin is built from LATAM for the world. The founder is Argentine and has lived the payment friction directly: local QR behavior, card-fee pain for merchants, exchange-rate gaps, and the reality of visitors who cannot pay like locals.

Cachin is also building defensibility beyond the app: onboarded local merchants, merchant discounts, and a founder-built Cachin POS that generates QR payments. The self-custodial cold-wallet/Visa card direction is a separate product line being worked on with a partner; Cachin can be the first wallet implementation, but it is not required for the core Cachin payment wedge.

  • Technical execution: show mobile app, QR routing, KYC state, pre-payment confirmation, payment proof, support path, and optional POS flow.

Competition

CacaoCash publicly positions around paying any QR in Latin America like a local. This validates the market and raises urgency. Cachin should differentiate by showing LATAM QR scope, the Argentina MVP demo, Sumsub KYC path, Crisp support, local founder execution, and merchant upgrade rails. Do not accuse competitors of copying in the public submission; use the safer and stronger line: built from LATAM for the world.

Claim-Safe Language

  • Say "active conversations with Fiserv/Clover", not "partnership," unless written confirmation exists.
  • Say "P2P.me live demo rail, Manteca planned scale-up subject to funding", not "live in 15 countries via Manteca," until paid Manteca coverage and production access are proven.
  • Say "separate card product line / first Cachin wallet implementation", not "global Visa card," until the card submission is ready.
  • Say "Sumsub KYC for supported local QR payments" and "lower-friction direct transfers for onboarded merchants, subject to limits/compliance", not broad "no KYC."
  • Say "Built from LATAM for the world", not public copying accusations.
