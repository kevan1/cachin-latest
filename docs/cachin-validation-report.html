Crypto Necessity Gut Check

What breaks if blockchain is removed? The product becomes a harder-to-scale Wise/Revolut/local-wallet bridge. It could still exist as fintech middleware, but it loses the strongest reason for users to start with Cachin: global stablecoin funding, crypto income spending, Solana/Seeker distribution, and faster merchant settlement paths.

Conclusion: crypto is necessary, but it must stay invisible. Users should not buy the product because it is crypto. They should buy it because it lets them pay locally when cards, cash, and local accounts fail.

Integration vs Build

Cachin should stay integration-first. A custom Solana program is not required for the MVP. The fastest path is existing stablecoin/token infrastructure, wallet abstraction, provider rails like Manteca or P2P.me, and optional Solana payment primitives for Cachin-owned QR/POS flows. Custom on-chain logic only becomes justified later for privacy rails, fee capture, compliance hooks, or card/cold-wallet product mechanics.

LayerRecommendation
User wallet and gas abstractionIntegrate existing wallet/passkey and fee-abstraction tooling; do not make users manage SOL.
Stablecoin payment / POS QRUse existing SPL token, Solana Pay-style, or Commerce Kit-style primitives for Cachin-owned merchant flows.
FX / local QR routingUse provider integrations first. Manteca/P2P.me-style rails are the product bottleneck, not a new smart contract.
Privacy railsRoadmap only. Do not pitch as live until there is a compliant design and demo.

Pivot or Persist

Persist, but narrow. Do not pivot away from Cachin. Pivot away from broad "we are the LATAM QR wallet" messaging. The validated wedge is:

Cachin lets tourists and foreigners pay supported local QRs across LATAM, proven first in Argentina, with merchant/POS upgrade rails and Solana/Seeker-native distribution.

If the Argentina QR demo cannot be recorded within the next proof sprint, downgrade the pitch to a narrower onboarded-merchant/POS demo and stop claiming broad QR utility until the provider route is proven.

7-Day Validation Sprint

  1. Day 1: Record one end-to-end Argentina MVP QR payment: fund balance, scan supported local QR, show FX/fees/final total, pay, receipt, support CTA.
  2. Day 2: Fill docs/proof/providers/rails-proof.md with Manteca trial screenshots, pricing/coverage proof, and P2P.me fallback evidence.
  3. Day 3: Fill docs/proof/support/support-and-refunds.md with Sumsub and Crisp screenshots plus refund/pending/failure copy.
  4. Day 4: Add at least three merchants to docs/proof/merchants/merchant-list.md; get one public/anonymized proof artifact.
  5. Day 5: Run five user interviews: two tourists/foreigners, one digital nomad, one crypto-paid freelancer, one merchant. Ask how they pay today and what breaks.
  6. Day 6: Publish a small waitlist/demo page with one CTA: "Pay like a local in LATAM, starting in Argentina." Measure signups and demo requests.
  7. Day 7: Re-score. If no live demo and no user/merchant proof, keep building but do not submit broad claims.

Final Recommendation

Go, proof-gated. Cachin is worth continuing because the pain is real, the founder fit is strong, the MVP is not imaginary, and the category is clearly moving. But the market has already noticed the same opportunity. The submission will only land if it is evidence-heavy and specific: LATAM scope, Argentina MVP proof, clear KYC/support, merchant upgrade rails, and a simple explanation of why Cachin wins despite similar products.
