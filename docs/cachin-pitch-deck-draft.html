Problem

LATAM is QR-first. Foreign money is not.

  • Tourists and foreigners cannot easily use local QR apps.
  • Freelancers get paid in crypto but spend locally.
  • Merchants show Mercado Pago, MODO, or other local QRs. Foreigners get stuck.
Make it concrete: a traveler has dollars/euros/crypto; the merchant wants QR or local settlement.

Who it serves

One network, two sides.

Core users

Tourists and foreigners first, then digital nomads, freelancers paid in crypto, Seeker users, and regular consumers who never need to see the crypto rails.

Merchant upgrade

Merchants do not need to onboard for basic QR payments. If they do, they unlock lower fees, instant settlement, discounts, POS tools, and direct confirmation.

This is the biggest upgrade from V1: Cachin is a two-sided payment network, not just a wallet.

Why crypto

Crypto stays behind the curtain.

  • Users get global funding and local spending.
  • Freelancers spend crypto income without cash-out loops.
  • Tourists scan local QRs without needing a local bank account.
  • Onboarded merchants get better economics than card rails.
  • Regular consumers are onboarded into crypto without needing to understand it.
